Traceworks

Active 2025–

Most PCB design services charge agency rates for work that doesn't require agency overhead. Traceworks was built to fix that. We're a small studio out of Nashua, New Hampshire, offering production-grade PCB layout at fixed, transparent prices — no retainers, no scope creep, no surprises.

I handle the embedded systems and firmware side: making sure the boards we deliver actually work in the real world, not just in simulation. That means thinking through signal integrity, power rail noise, and manufacturability constraints from the start, not as an afterthought before the Gerbers go out. Every design we ship is IPC-2221 and IPC-7351 compliant, DRC-verified for JLCPCB and PCBWay, and delivered as KiCad native files with full ownership transferred to the client.

Beyond client work, we've built a small suite of KiCad plugins — AutoBOM, TraceCheck, and SchemaSnap — and a couple of AI agents for PCB design called LayoutBot and SpecWriter. The plugins automate the parts of the workflow that are tedious and error-prone. The agents are more experimental, but the direction feels right: most of the friction in PCB design is documentation and iteration, not creativity.

The team is four people. Prithvi Gupta leads the PCB design work. Anghelo Araujo Lazaro handles the software infrastructure. Vandan Brahmbhatt runs operations and growth. We're still early, but we've built something real and clients are showing up.

What We Deliver

Every project ships with Gerber files, Excellon drill files, a BOM, pick-and-place coordinates, schematic PDF, and 3D renders. For complex boards — 4–6 layers with RF, power, or impedance-controlled traces — we get on a call first to make sure the requirements are locked before layout begins. We also offer 48-hour express turnaround, fab coordination, and component sourcing as add-ons.
