Engineering YouTube Channel

Active 2023–present

I started the channel because I kept facing the same issue. Good engineering content is either too basic or filled with language that assumes you already know everything. I wanted to create videos I would have enjoyed when I was just starting out.

The focus is mostly on robotics, embedded systems, and autonomous navigation. I'm sharing whatever I'm working on or thinking about. I never aimed to build an audience. I just wanted the content to be genuinely helpful.

At some point, people from other countries began watching. I still don't completely understand how that happened. But if someone created something because of a video I made, that means more to me than any number on a dashboard.

I also try to make it actually enjoyable to watch. Engineering content doesn't have to be dry. If I can make someone laugh while learning how a fuselage works, that's a win-win situation.
